About Property Clearance Division

The Property Clearance Division (PCD) is a branch of the U.S. General Services Administration. We partner with government agencies, municipalities, and authorized contractors to manage the recovery, removal, transportation, and documentation of personal property and assets from federal and non-federal sites.

Founded in 1971, PCD operates six regional offices across the continental United States, serving thousands of federal and interagency clients annually.

Mission Statement

The Property Clearance Division (PCD) is committed to the safe, professional, and responsible management of federal and interagency property assets. We serve the public interest by ensuring that property recovery, documentation, and clearance operations are conducted with integrity, accountability, and strict adherence to federal regulatory standards.

Our Core Mission: To provide federal, state, and local partners with comprehensive property recovery, documentation, and evidence handling services — safely, professionally, and responsibly.

History & Background

The Property Clearance Division was established in 1971 under the General Services Administration in response to the growing need for a dedicated federal body to manage interagency property disposition and asset clearance. In its early decades, PCD focused primarily on surplus federal property removal from decommissioned military installations and government facilities.

Through the 1980s and 1990s, PCD's mandate expanded significantly to include media transfer operations — specifically the cataloguing and preservation of analog media from government facilities — as well as hazardous material identification protocols and chain-of-custody documentation for sensitive property.

Today, PCD operates six regional offices across the United States and maintains active partnerships with federal agencies, municipal governments, and authorized private contractors. The division employs field specialists, documentation analysts, logistics coordinators, and administrative personnel across all regions.

Core Values

  • Integrity — Every asset is documented and handled according to federal guidelines with no exceptions.
  • Accountability — Chain-of-custody is maintained at every stage of the recovery and clearance process.
  • Professionalism — Our field teams are trained, certified, and equipped to manage complex property scenarios.
  • Service — We exist to serve our agency partners and the communities we operate in.

Organizational Structure

PCD is organized into five primary operational divisions: Field Operations, Asset Documentation & Inventory, Media Transfer Operations, Archival Records Management, and Hazard & Safety Compliance. Each regional office maintains its own Field Operations team while sharing centralized documentation and compliance oversight from national headquarters in Washington, D.C.

PCD Services

Our Service Areas

The Property Clearance Division offers a comprehensive suite of federally authorized property and asset management services to partner agencies, municipalities, and authorized contractors.

🏚️ Property Recovery & Cleanout

PCD field teams conduct systematic recovery and cleanout operations at residential, commercial, and government facilities. Services include full-property inventory, item categorization, safe removal, and site documentation. All operations comply with federal property disposition standards and applicable state regulations.

🚛 Asset Recovery & Transport

We coordinate the logistics of large-scale asset recovery and transportation for government surplus, estate resolution, and interagency transfer operations. Our fleet of GSA-certified transport vehicles operates across all six regional service areas with full chain-of-custody documentation.

📼 Media Transfer Operations

The Media Transfer Office (MTO) handles the cataloguing, secure transfer, and archival processing of analog and digital media from government facilities. This includes magnetic tape, film, microfilm, and digital storage media. The MTO maintains strict handling protocols for sensitive and classified media.

📁 Archival Review & Records

PCD works in conjunction with the National Archives and Records Administration (NARA) to process, review, and prepare documentary records for long-term federal archival storage. Our document specialists are trained in federal records classification standards.

🛡️ Hazard & Safety Management

Properties containing hazardous materials, environmental contamination, or safety-risk items require specialized handling. PCD's certified hazard response teams coordinate with EPA and OSHA to ensure compliant identification, containment, and removal of risk materials prior to full property clearance.

To request PCD services for a federal or interagency project, contact your nearest regional office or submit a Service Request Form.

Operational Divisions

The Property Clearance Division is organized into five core operational divisions, each responsible for a distinct aspect of the agency's mission.

Field Operations

Front-Line Recovery

Coordinates all on-site property recovery, cleanout, and inspection activities. Field Operations teams are deployed from regional offices and operate under strict safety and documentation protocols.

Asset Documentation & Inventory

Records & Chain-of-Custody

Responsible for the creation, maintenance, and verification of all property inventory records. Ensures compliance with federal asset tracking and reporting requirements.

Media Transfer Operations

MTO — Analog & Digital Media

Manages the cataloguing, secure handling, and transfer of media assets from government and interagency facilities. Includes classification review for sensitive media.

Restricted Access

Archival Records Management

Long-Term Documentation

Oversees the processing and preparation of federal documentary records for NARA archival submission. Works in coordination with agency records officers.

Hazard & Safety Compliance

Environmental & Safety Standards

Ensures all PCD field operations meet EPA, OSHA, and GSA safety standards. Conducts hazardous material assessments and coordinates remediation activities.

The Property Clearance Division offers meaningful federal careers with competitive pay, comprehensive benefits, and the satisfaction of public service. PCD employees work across the United States helping communities and government agencies manage complex property clearance operations.

All PCD positions are federal civil service appointments. Applications are processed through USAJobs.gov in coordination with the GSA Human Capital Office.

How to Apply

  1. Create a USAJobs Profile

    All federal applications are submitted via USAJobs.gov. Create or update your profile before applying to PCD positions.

  2. Review Position Requirements

    Read the full position description including required qualifications, duty location, and salary range. PCD positions may require background investigation clearance.

  3. Submit Application

    Submit your résumé, transcripts, and any required supporting documents through USAJobs. Incomplete applications will not be considered.

  4. Background & Suitability Review

    Selected candidates will undergo a federal background investigation appropriate to the position's security requirements.

  5. Onboarding & Training

    New hires complete a formal onboarding program including PCD orientation, safety training, and position-specific field certification.
